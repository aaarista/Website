Final Project Introduction

In the last decade, commuter habits have been a central focus for New York City's elected officials. One important strategy has been on the emphasis of using bicycles as a means of transportation to get around the city. New York City has infused a great deal of budget dollars into the investments necessary to make the city more bike friendly as well as safe for cycylists. Because the commuter logistics of a city play such a large roll in shaping a city and how it functions, I have choosen to make this the focus of this project.

Project Focus

There will be three(3) primary areas of focus for the research and reporting to follow:

  1. Pedestrian cyclists crossing the three main bridges in downtown Manhattan: Williamburg Bridge, Queensboro Bridge, Manhattan Bridge.
  2. Analysis of Citi Bike data from the primary locations in key locations of high traffic areas with heavy adoption of cycling as a way of moving around the city.
  3. Analysis of changes and response of the choosen locations as well emerging areas with high commuter cyclist activity.

Primary Sources of Data and Data Wrangling/Analysis Tools

The two(2) resources for collecitng data are NYC Open Data and Citi Bike's data website source. Also, the Vision Zero website was used as an initial source for ideas on layout and research methods. For the wrangling, aggregating, charting, and analysis of data; Jupyter Notebook was the tool that was employed for this project.
